PCSD ADMINISTRATIVE ORDER NO. 12 Series of 2011

Section 3.

Wildlife Collector’s Permit – a permit to take or collect from the wild certain species and quantities of wildlife for commercial purposes;

Section 19.1.

Qualification. Qualification. Only Filipino citizens, or corporations, partnerships, cooperatives or associations 60% of the capital of which is owned by Filipinos, shall be allowed to collect non-threatened economically important species for direct trade purposes.

Requirements
  1. Duly accomplished application form with 2 recent 2”x2” photo.
  2. List of species to be collected indicating quantity for each and methods of collection to be used.
  3. For aquatic wildlife, in case of incidental catches that can no longer be returned to the wild, an Affidavit of Undertaking that the applicant shall submit an inventory of incidental catches within 15 days after collection.
  4. Clearances from affected communities (as applicable):
    1. Free & Prior Informed Consent (FPIC).
    2. Barangay Clearance.
    3. Municipal/City Clearance.
    4. Protected Areas and Mgt. Bureau if inside a protected area
    5. Consent of individuals/associations with valid tenurial or other private rights and/or
  5. Names, addresses and photocopies of any valid ID photo and signature of authorized collectors/trappers
  6. Payment of fee – 500.00 PHP
